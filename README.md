For 130 years, Pierre Curie's 1894 dissymmetry principle has sat in the physics literature as a law about crystals: a physical effect cannot have more symmetry than its cause. Researchers applied it to quartz. To piezoelectric ceramics. To ferroelectrics. No one applied it to life.

**DISSYMMETRY** applies it to everything.

Across five distinct spatial scales — from the Ångström geometry of a DNA polymerase active site to the millisecond commitment timing of a cell choosing its fate — the same structural condition recurs without exception. Every information boundary in biology is a Curie dissymmetry. Every quantum effect that biology harnesses, every regulatory checkpoint that chromatin enforces, every spontaneous oscillation that a gene network sustains, every flexoelectric field that a membrane generates: each is, precisely and formally, the biological implementation of a principle that Pierre Curie wrote down while compressing quartz crystals in a Paris laboratory thirteen decades ago.

This is the claim of DISSYMMETRY. It is not a metaphor.

## Why This Has Not Been Said Before

The unification requires holding five distinct bodies of recent science in simultaneous view — and recognizing that their most fundamental results are the same result at different magnifications.

**Quantum biology** confirmed in December 2022 (Slocombe, Al-Khalili, Sacchi, *Journal of Physical Chemistry Letters*) that proton tunneling at the wobble position of a DNA codon is enhanced one hundredfold by the specific geometry of the polymerase active site. The finding was recognized as a landmark in quantum biology. It was not recognized as a piezoelectric effect — the mechanical analogue of what Curie measured in quartz in 1880.

**Membrane biophysics** established flexoelectricity in lipid bilayers (Petrov, 2002; Ahmadpoor and Sharma, 2015): the coupling of membrane curvature to electric polarization. The effect was measured, parameterized, and filed under soft condensed matter physics. It was not connected to time crystals.

**Chromatin biology** produced the fractal globule model of genome organization (Lieberman-Aiden et al., *Science*, 2009) and the topologically associating domain architecture (Rao et al., 2014; Fudenberg et al., 2016). The fractal structure was noted as elegant. It was not recognized as a photonic crystal with a biological spectral bandgap governing transcription factor diffusion.

**Time crystal physics** observed the first continuous time crystal in a quantum atom-cavity system (Kongkhambut et al., *Science*, 2022). The biological analogue — a gene ensemble whose entropy synchronizes spontaneously during cell-fate transitions with a freely chosen time phase — appeared independently in the genomics literature (Tsuchiya, Yoshikawa, Giuliani, *bioRxiv*, 2026) without the connection to time-translation symmetry breaking being named.

**Self-organized criticality in gene regulation** produced the Maxwell's demon framework for cell fate (Tsuchiya, Yoshikawa, Giuliani, *International Journal of Molecular Sciences*, May 2025). The demon was identified. Its location at a fractal photonic bandgap edge was not.

DISSYMMETRY is the first work to traverse all five simultaneously — and to show that traversal is not optional. The five are not independent domains with interesting structural analogies. They are one domain, one broken symmetry, observed at five scales of biological organization with five different instruments.

## What Is New, Field by Field

### Against All of Quantum Biology

Quantum biology has understood, since Löwdin (1963) and now confirmed by Slocombe et al. (2021, 2022), that proton tunneling shapes mutation rates. The field's frontier question is *how much* tunneling, *where*, and *when*.

DISSYMMETRY answers *why position 3*. The wobble position of the codon is not merely a structurally looser base-pairing site. It is the location in the genetic code where the polymerase active site geometry is non-centrosymmetric in the precise sense that Curie's theorem requires for a vectorial effect. The 64-to-20 codon surjection — the degeneracy of the genetic code — is the quantum error-correcting kernel of a biological piezoelectric crystal, maintained by 4.2 billion years of selection to concentrate dominant quantum noise into the code's non-information-bearing dimension. No current quantum biology framework has this structure. The Yale–Google–UCSB parametric oscillator result (*PRX Quantum*, April 2026), demonstrating that barrier asymmetry modulation produces hundredfold tunneling rate enhancement in a simulable quantum system, now makes the piezoelectric coefficient of the genetic code experimentally tractable for the first time.

### Against All of Crystal Physics

Piezoelectricity and flexoelectricity in biology have been documented at the organismal scale — bone, collagen, cell membranes — since Fukada and Yasuda (1957). The documentation has been thorough and the mechanisms well-characterized. What has not been done is to identify the biological piezoelectric crystal at the most fundamental scale: the genetic code itself, with its non-centrosymmetric active site geometry as the polar axis and the tautomeric proton displacement as the mechanical-to-electrical transduction event.

The ATP synthase F₀ c-ring — Boyer's molecular machine (Nobel Lecture, 1997) — is simultaneously a piezoelectric transducer at the molecular scale, a chemiosmotic boundary at the thermodynamic scale, and a quantum proton transfer device at the Ångström scale. Three frameworks in the physics literature describe different aspects of the same machine. DISSYMMETRY identifies the Curie dissymmetry — the chiral non-centrosymmetric geometry of the c-ring — as the structural condition that makes all three descriptions simultaneously correct and mutually necessary.

### Against All of Membrane Biophysics

The flexoelectric coupling in lipid bilayers is well-measured (f ≈ 1–10 × 10⁻¹⁸ C). Its physiological consequences have been studied in the context of membrane mechanosensitivity. DISSYMMETRY extends the analysis in two directions that have not been taken.

The first: at sites of high bilayer curvature — cristae junctions, endocytic vesicle necks, ER tubular networks — the flexoelectric coupling generates local electric fields (10⁵–10⁶ V/m) sufficient to modulate quantum proton transfer rates at membrane-embedded enzymes. The curvature parameter of LUZZATI's liquid crystal model and the piezoelectric coefficient of PIEZO's crystal model are the same boundary condition in two languages.

The second: the lipid bilayer's GHz-frequency dielectric fluctuations, coupled to its flexoelectric non-centrosymmetry, constitute a proton-transport photonic time crystal — a medium whose time-modulated permittivity opens momentum bandgaps in the proton dispersion relation, exponentially amplifying quantum proton transfer within those bandgaps. The photonic time crystal, first demonstrated at microwave frequencies (Wang et al., *Science Advances*, 2023; Lin and Zhang, *Nature Communications*, 2025) and approaching optical frequencies in 2025–2026, is realized in every living cell's mitochondrial membrane at the frequency of proton transport. The bicontinuous cubic phases of the endoplasmic reticulum, identified as topological gyroid photonic crystals by Lu et al. (*Nature Photonics*, 2013), extend the same principle to three-dimensional proton waveguiding. The Zhao–Smalyukh liquid-crystal space-time crystal (*Nature Materials*, 2025) is the closest classical experimental analogue of the same physics.

### Against All of Chromatin Biology

The fractal globule model and TAD architecture of the genome are among the most-studied structural features in modern cell biology. Super-resolution chromatin tracing (Bintu et al., *Science*, 2018), cohesin-depletion experiments (Rao et al., *Cell*, 2017), and loop extrusion modeling (Fudenberg et al., 2016) have established the hierarchical architecture in quantitative detail.

What has not been established is the information-theoretic consequence of the fractal topology for transcription factor diffusion. The spectral dimension of the fractal chromatin (d_S ≈ 1.37, substantially below the Hausdorff dimension d_H ≈ 2.5) governs the anomalous diffusion exponent of transcription factors in living nuclei. The hundredfold reduction in effective diffusion coefficient observed experimentally is a consequence of fractal topology, not viscosity — a distinction with experimental signatures that existing models do not capture. The CTCF-cohesin loop extrusion mechanism is the biological iterated function system of the chromatin fractal: each loop extrusion event is one step of the fractal construction algorithm, and the directional asymmetry of CTCF binding sites is the Curie dissymmetry that drives the construction toward fractal rather than random polymer geometry. Cohesin-depleted cells exhibit the d = 0 degeneration: the fractal bandgap collapses, compartment structure persists (the large-scale partition survives), but the spectral gap at the TAD scale disappears.

### Against All of Time Crystal Physics

Time crystal physics has progressed from Wilczek's original proposal (2012) through quantum discrete time crystals on quantum processors (Google AI Quantum, *Nature*, 2021) to continuous time crystals in atom-cavity systems (Kongkhambut et al., *Science*, 2022) to photonic time crystals approaching optical frequencies (2024–2026). The three structural types — discrete (periodically driven, many-body-localized), continuous (spontaneous, dissipatively stabilized), and amplifying (permittivity-modulated, k-gap) — correspond to Wilczek's Rigidity, Spontaneity, and Amplification axes.

The **CP gene ensemble** is a biological continuous time crystal: a spontaneously oscillating gene regulatory system whose entropy synchronizes with the whole-expression state during cell-fate transitions, with a freely chosen commitment time phase distributed uniformly across individual cells under identical stimulation — the canonical signature of spontaneous time-translation symmetry breaking, observed without a periodic drive.

The **lipid bilayer** is a biological amplifying (photonic) time crystal: a dielectrically time-modulated medium whose GHz flexoelectric permittivity oscillations open proton momentum bandgaps and exponentially amplify quantum proton transfer within those bandgaps.

No prior work has identified any of the three in biology, let alone their simultaneous co-realization in every eukaryotic cell as a closed architectural unit. The CP gene ensemble was described as a Maxwell's demon by Tsuchiya, Yoshikawa, and Giuliani (2026) without the time-crystal identification.
